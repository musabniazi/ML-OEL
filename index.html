Project Overview

What This Project Does

This project applies a complete end-to-end machine learning pipeline to the IBM Telco Customer Churn dataset — 7,043 customer records with 21 features covering demographics, subscribed services, contract details, and billing information.

The supervised learning component trains and benchmarks five classifiers to predict which customers are likely to cancel their service. The best model — Random Forest — achieves 80.26% accuracy and is recommended for production deployment as a live monthly churn-scoring engine integrated into the company's CRM.

The unsupervised clustering component discovers three natural customer segments: high-risk newcomers, loyal long-term customers, and a mid-tier transitional group. These segments enable targeted, differentiated retention campaigns rather than one-size-fits-all marketing.

Unsupervised Learning

Customer Segmentation

Three clustering algorithms applied to discover natural customer groups without using the churn label.

🎯
K-Means Clustering
K = 3 (Elbow Method)

The Elbow Method on K=1..10 identified 3 as the optimal number of clusters. Produces the most interpretable and actionable customer segments. Clusters visualized in 2D using PCA dimensionality reduction.

Cluster 0
High Risk
~45% churn
Cluster 1
Loyal
~12% churn
Cluster 2
Mid-Tier
~27% churn
🌳
Agglomerative Hierarchical
Ward Linkage · 3 Clusters

Ward linkage minimizes within-cluster variance. A dendrogram was plotted on a 300-sample subset to confirm the cut point at 3 clusters. Results broadly corroborate K-Means, validating the 3-group customer structure.

Key Characteristics
✦ No cluster shape assumption
✦ Dendrogram provides exploratory hierarchy
✦ O(n²) complexity — best for exploration
✦ Confirms K-Means segment structure
🔍
DBSCAN
eps=0.5 · min_samples=5

Density-based algorithm that auto-determines cluster count and flags outliers as noise (label = -1). Useful for identifying atypical customers who don't fit any standard segment and may require individual case-by-case review.

DBSCAN Output
✦ Clusters: auto-detected (1–2 dense regions)
✦ Noise points: significant fraction flagged
✦ Best use: outlier / anomaly detection
✦ High-dim data causes density diffusion

Business Insights

Key Findings & Recommendations

Data-driven insights translated into actionable strategies for the telecom company.

⏱️
Early-Tenure Risk Window
Most churn occurs in the first 0–12 months. New customers need proactive engagement before dissatisfaction solidifies.
📋
Contract Type is Critical
Month-to-month customers churn at 3× the rate of two-year contract holders. Long-term commitment dramatically reduces churn.
💸
Price vs Perceived Value
Fiber optic subscribers pay the most yet churn the most — a clear value perception gap that bundled services can bridge.
💳
Payment Method Signal
Electronic check users churn at ~45% vs ~15–18% for auto-pay customers. Friction in payment experience predicts disengagement.
🛡️
Value-Added Services Retain
OnlineSecurity and TechSupport negatively correlate with churn — customers who use these services feel more invested in the platform.

5 Actionable Recommendations

1
Launch Early-Life Loyalty Programme Discounts and onboarding support in months 1–6 for all new customers (Cluster 0)
-15–20% churn
2
Incentivise Annual Contract Migration 10–15% discount to convert month-to-month subscribers to longer commitments
3× lower churn
3
Bundle Value-Added Services for Fiber Optic Include OnlineSecurity + TechSupport to justify premium pricing for high-charge customers
Value uplift
4
Electronic Check Migration Campaign Offer $3–$5 monthly credit to switch from e-check to auto-pay via bank transfer or card
-10pp churn
5
Deploy Random Forest as Live Churn Scorer Integrate model into CRM for monthly risk scoring — flag P(churn) > 0.55 for retention outreach
